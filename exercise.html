Stay Active with Exercise

Regular physical activity improves strength, endurance, mood, and overall health.

Why Exercise Matters

Physical activity helps control weight, reduces the risk of heart disease, strengthens bones and muscles, improves mental health, and boosts energy levels. Even moderate exercise can make a big difference in daily life.

Types of Exercise

  • Aerobic (Cardio): Activities that increase heart rate (e.g., walking, running, swimming, cycling).
  • Strength Training: Builds muscle and bone density (e.g., weight lifting, bodyweight exercises like push-ups and squats).
  • Flexibility: Improves range of motion (e.g., stretching, yoga).
  • Balance: Helps prevent falls, especially important as we age (e.g., tai chi, standing on one foot).

Recommendations and Tips

Health experts recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week, plus muscle-strengthening activities on two or more days.

  1. Start small – even a 10-minute walk counts.
  2. Choose activities you enjoy to stay motivated.
  3. Mix different types for balanced fitness.
  4. Warm up before and cool down after exercising.
  5. Listen to your body and rest when needed.

Making exercise a regular habit leads to long-term health benefits and greater well-being.
